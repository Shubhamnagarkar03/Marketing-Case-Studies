01 / Customer Engagement

The Flatline Era

Online food and grocery apps peaked in 2021 riding a lockdown tailwind. Since then, combined monthly transacting users for Zomato and Swiggy have barely moved. Meanwhile, India's kirana universe — 12 million stores serving 97 crore shoppers — didn't blink. The apps got bigger in headlines; the streets stayed the same.

35.4M combined app MTU in FY25 Q3 — vs 20.3M just two quarters prior. Looks like growth. Is actually a plateau at 2.5% of India.

02 / Market Share

Big Numbers,
Tiny Slice

India's online grocery market grew from ₹42B in 2018 to over ₹1,100B by 2024. Impressive, until you remember that India's total grocery retail is worth ₹130,000B+ annually. Online's share? Still crawling under 1%. The headline number is real. The dominance is a press release.

<1% of India's grocery retail happens online — despite ₹1,100B in annual online grocery GMV and years of VC-funded discounts.

03 / The Fee Architecture

The Small Cart
Is a Big Con

Buy one pack of biscuits online and watch what happens. The platforms layer delivery fees, small cart fees, handling fees, and platform fees on top until your ₹29 snack costs ₹90+. This isn't a bug — it's engineered. The "small cart fee" exists to push you to add more items, lifting average order value for the platform. Your kirana charges none of this. Ever.

400% increase in Zomato/Swiggy platform fees between 2023 and 2024. Restaurant menus on apps are now 20–30% pricier than dine-in.

04 / Public Health

You Didn't Just
Stop Going Out.

When the bhaji wala lost to a 10-minute delivery, something else got lost too: the walk. The interaction. The decision. A 2025 Cureus study from Kolkata found that frequent food delivery app users showed significantly higher rates of sedentary behaviour, overeating, and emotional eating compared to low-frequency and in-store buyers. The convenience economy has a hidden invoice.

81% of frequent food delivery app users in the study linked their app usage to a more sedentary lifestyle. 46% were classified as obese vs 31% in low-frequency users.

05 / Verdict

Which Is
Feasible?

Online delivery is genuinely useful — for a narrow, urban, time-pressed 5–8% of India who can absorb the fee stack and don't mind being nudged into larger baskets. For everyone else, the kirana isn't a legacy system. It's the incumbent with better margins, human credit, zero delivery fees, and 12 million locations.

The sector built extraordinary infrastructure. But infrastructure isn't habit. Zomato's flatline MTU, Swiggy's widening losses, ONDC's transaction collapse the moment subsidies dried up — these aren't bumps. They're the market telling the truth.

Until platforms solve pricing parity, Tier 2+ viability, and a vendor ecosystem that isn't held hostage by 30–40% commissions, the bhaji wala outside your building remains the most efficient last-mile commerce system India has ever built.

"They disrupted the supply chain. The kirana disrupted nothing — and won."
